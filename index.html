Women Involved in Software and Hardware

What is WISH? WISH is a club at Cal Poly with two goals: provide a support system for women in computing at Cal Poly and bridge the gender gap in Computer Science.

Why I love WISH: Being a part WISH has completely shaped my experience in college. I can honestly say I would not be majoring in Computer Science if it wasn’t for this club. As a girl in Computer Science it’s easy to feel like I don’t belong. It’s intimidating when I walk into a class and I’m one of three girls. And while I absolutely love coding, in these kinds of situations sometimes I question my decision to major in Computer Science. But WISH changes this; by surrounding myself with a community of passionate women, I actually feel like I belong.

My role in WISH: Not only has WISH provided me with a support system, but as Community Outreach Director I was given an outlet towards sharing my passion for Computer Science with others, especially younger girls. Through WISH:

- I've been in charge of organizing Pacecho Code Club, a six week program that occurs once a quarter where WISH teaches 2nd-4th graders how to code using curriculum from Code.org.
- I've arranged and participated in local high school visits to speak to students and promote Computer Science.
- I've implemented a new outreach activity that allows students to learn about circuitry by physically drawing circuits on a piece of paper.
- But by far, the most rewarding outreach event I've planned was Expanding Engineering. Expanding Engineering was a day-long event where WISH brought 120 underprivileged high school students to Cal Poly to participate in five different student-run workshops to learn about Computer Science (http://www.calpoly.edu/~wish/pages/expandengineering.html). To be able to show these students that Computer Science is fun, exciting, and most importantly attainable for them, is the reason I have devoted and will continue to devote hours my time towards WISH and its cause.
